Burning - Biomass

Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions from burning of biomass consist of methane and nitrous oxide gases from biomass combustion of forest land cover classes ‘Humid and Tropical Forest’ and ‘Other Forests’, and of methane, nitrous oxide, and carbon dioxide gases from combustion of organic soils. Emissions are computed at Tier 1 following the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National GHG Inventories. They are available by country, with global coverage and relative to the period 1990 to present, with annual updates.
