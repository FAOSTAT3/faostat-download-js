Pesticides (Use)

< Back


The Statistics Division of the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations started the collection of data on consumption of major individual pesticides products about three decades ago. However, the response to the related Pesticides Consumption Annual Questionnaire sent to all member countries was not very encouraging. Therefore, in 1986 in co-operation with the Commission of the European Union, a study was undertaken to find ways to improve the country coverage of the data. The present work of collecting data on groups of pesticides is a result of the recommendations of this study. Data collected earlier have been published in various issues of the Production Yearbook. The present database refers to the quantity of pesticides used in or sold to the agricultural sector for crops and seeds; data are expressed in metric tons of active ingredients. Information on quantities applied to single crops is not available.

Description of Elements


Use (tonnes of active ingredients)


This refers to quantities of pesticides applied to crops and seeds in the agriculture sector. Figures are expressed in metric tons of active ingredients. However, due to some country reporting practices, the data may be reported by: use in formulated product; sales; distribution or imports for use in the agricultural sector. In these cases it is specified in the country notes.


Note regarding the selected parameters:


The greater sign after World "World >" implies that the extraction information is requested for the countries available in the database.


In the case of item selection, for example, "Insecticides +" the plus sign indicates that the extraction is requesting information on the aggregate only for each main category (Insecticides, Mineral oils, Herbicides, Fungicides, Seed treatments Insecticides, Seed treatments Fungicides, Plant growth regulators and Rodenticides). This option does not provide the detailed break-down.


In the case of item selection, for example, "Insecticides >" the greater sign indicates that the extraction is requesting information on the detailed break-down for each main category (Insecticides, Herbicides, Fungicides, Seed treatments Insecticides, Seed treatments Fungicides, Plant growth regulators and Rodenticides) excluding the aggregated figures. Please, note that some countries did not report breakdown for some items.

    • LIMITATIONS OF THE DATABASE

    A strict inter-country comparison on the basis of the database is not feasible because:

  • The country coverage and time series are incomplete due to a high rate of non-response.

  • Although countries have been requested to report data in terms of active ingredients, some countries may have reported in formulation weight (including diluents and adjuvants) without specific indication.

    ALGERIA

    Data refer to sales for use in the agricultural sector expressed in Formulated Products.


    AUSTRIA
    Data refer to sales for application to crops and seeds in agriculture. "INSECTICIDES" data include insecticide seed treatments, acaricides, molluscicides, nematicides and synergists. "FUNGICIDES & BACTERICIDES" data include fungicide seed treatments. "OTHER FUNGICIDES" data refer to the amount of Sulfur and Copper products.


    BAHAMAS
    Data refer to Imports expressed in Formulated Products. "FUNGICIDES & BACTERICIDES" data include fungicide seed treatments. "OTHER PESTICIDES" data include mineral oils.

    BAHRAIN

    BANGLADESH
    In the period 2001-2008 the data are expressed in Formulated Products.

    BELGIUM
    Data refer to sales for application to crops and seeds in agriculture. "HERBICIDES" figures also include quantity of herbicides sold for non-agricultural use.

    BHUTAN
    Data refer to total Imports of Formulated Products.

    BOLIVIA
    Data refer to imports in formulated products.

    BURKINA FASO
    Data in the years 1998 and 2005-2009 are expressed in Formulated Products.

    CAMEROON
    In the periods 1990-1992 and 2002-2010 data refer to quantities of Formulated Products imported into the country for both agricultural and non-agricultural use.

    CANADA
    Data refer to pesticide sales for use in the agricultural sector. Data are obtained from different sources as follows: (a) 1990 and 1994 data - FAO Annual questionnaires returned by the Commercial Chemical Evaluation Branch, Environment Canada; (b) 2006 data - OECD AgroEnvironmental indicators 2008, Secretariat estimates based on CropLife Canada sales data in dollar value, http://www.oecd.org/dataoecd/56/23/41255459.xls; (c) 2008 data - Pest Control Products, Sales Report 2007-2008 http://www.hc-sc.gc.ca/cps-spc/pubs/pest/_corp-plan/sales-2007-2008-ventes/index-eng.php. As a result, a strict data comparison is not feasible. Also, please note that according to the “Pest Control Products Sales Report 2007-2008”, a product may have a number of different uses on the label. As the sales reporting does not collect data on the relative amount of a product used for a specific label use, the data may not necessarily be separated into only one product type. This means that there may be overlap between the product type groupings; thus these numbers should not be summed to obtain total quantities sold in Canada in 2008, in fact an over- reporting would occur.
