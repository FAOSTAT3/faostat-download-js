Burning Crop Residues


Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions from burning crop residues consist of methane and nitrous oxide gases produced by the combustion of a percentage of the crop residues burnt on-site. Computed at Tier 1 following the 2006 IPCC Guidelines for National GHG Inventories; available by country, with global coverage and relative to the period 1961 to present, with annual updates, and projections for 2030 and 2050.
