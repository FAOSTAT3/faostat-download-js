This table completes the information on producer prices in local currency. It reports, for each country, currency names and ISO currency codes starting from 1991. Users can track the currency in which prices are labelled every year through the "First year" and "Last year" columns whereas FAOSTAT series in Standard Local Currencies (SLC) give prices expressed in a unique reference currency throughout the period. Conversion rates between old and new currencies are given below for background information.

49 Cuba CUC Peso Cubano Convertible 2005 present   1 CUC = 25 CUP but 24 CUP = 1 CUC and 1CUC = 1.08 USD No exchange rates available for Cuba in the IMF, therefore UIC (Ufficio Italiano Cambi) and other sources were used instead.
CUP Peso Cubano 1991 2004     Cuban currency has no official value outside the country. The cuban peso (CUP) is used only by local citizens, whose wages are paid mostly in pesos, for everyday expenses. The exchange rate used by public sector for some accounting purposes is set at 1 CUP = 1 CUC. However, for practical purposes, the exchange rate applied by the Cuban banks and Cadecas (exchange bureaux) is different: from 18 March 2005, that rate has been set at 24:1 when exchanging CUC for CUP and 25:1 when going from CUP to CUC. The convertible peso is currently pegged at 1.08 USD and is used mainly by tourists for luxury products and services.
